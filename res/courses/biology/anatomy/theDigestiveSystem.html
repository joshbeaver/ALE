The Digestive System

The Human Digestive System

Structure and Function

Humans have a two-ended digestive tract (also called the alimentary canal or gastrointestinal tract). The digestive system can be divided into tubular alimentary canal and accessory organs.
The overall function of the all the parts of the digestive system is to convert food into energy and basic nutrients to feed the entire body.
The following processes occur withing the digestive tract of humans:

  • Ingestion- the intake of food from the mouth
  • Digestion - the mechanical and chemical breakdown of food
  • Absorption - the diffusion of simple food molecules through the intestinal wall into the blood
  • Assimilation - incorporation of nutrients such as amino acids and glucose into the cell
  • Egestion - the removal of undigested and unabsorbed waste from the body through the anus in the form of faeces

Summarised Structure and Function of Parts of The Digestive System

    The Mouth
    • Teeth - prepare food for digestion by cutting and grinding (Mechanical Break Down)
    • Tongue - forms floor of the mouth
    • Hard and Soft Palate - forms roof of the mouth
    • Stratified Epithelium - cells that line the mouth
    • 3 Pairs of Salivary Glands - produce saliva which assists the chemical breakdown of food
    • The Uvula - flap of tissue hanging from the soft palate which closes the internal nasal passage during swallowing
    The Pharynx
    • The pharynx is the area where the nasal passages and alimentary canal cross each other
    • The pharynx leads to the oesophagus and trachea
    • During swallowing the glottis is closed by the epiglottis
    The Oesophagus
    • The oesophagus is a tube that lies behind the trachea
    • It has thick muscular walls and is line with stratified epithelium cells
    • It conducts food to the stomach
    The Stomach
    • The stomach is a muscular bag-like organ
    • The stomach walls are very thick
    • At each opening the stomach has a valve like sphincter. The cardiac sphincter between the oesophagus and stomach
      and the pyloric sphincter between the stomach and the duodenum. The sphincters ensure one way movement of food
      and prevent reflux
